# Claim-Evidence Map

This file is an internal writing-control sheet. It keeps the manuscript aligned with a Nature-style evidence-first argument and prevents the draft from overstating what the current simulations prove.

| Claim | Current evidence | Status | Boundary to preserve |
|---|---|---|---|
| CBF filtering is the safety-enforcing component. | In `output/6dof_batch/summary.csv`, baseline gives negative `min_h` in all five 6-DOF scenarios, while CBF-based variants keep `min_h > 0`. | Supported by simulation. | Applies to tested static-obstacle scenarios and the implemented CBF-QP feasibility conditions. |
| The robust term mainly improves tracking under disturbance. | In the strong-disturbance scenario, `cbf_robust` reduces mean error from `0.162318` to `0.133425` compared with `cbf_only`. | Supported by ablation. | Do not claim it is the main source of safety-margin recovery. |
| The disturbance observer recovers safety margin and improves tracking. | In the strong-disturbance scenario, `cbf_observer` increases `min_h` from `0.008370` to `0.261990` and reduces mean error to `0.073981` compared with `cbf_only`. `output/6dof_batch/figures/disturbance_observer_diagnostic.png` shows true vs estimated disturbance; `observer_summary.csv` reports mean observer error `0.056066` against mean disturbance norm `0.254587` in the strong-disturbance case. | Supported by ablation and observer diagnostic. | Requires bounded disturbance-estimation error in the formal analysis. |
| The full controller gives the best tracking while preserving high safety margin. | In the strong-disturbance scenario, `cbf_observer_robust` gives `min_h=0.261435` and mean error `0.065852`. Across the batch, it has the lowest mean error among tested controllers. | Supported by current batch. | State "among the tested controllers", not universally best. |
| The controller is not merely tuned for one CBF gain or nominal actuator limit. | `output/6dof_robustness/robustness_summary.csv` sweeps CBF gain scale from `0.65` to `1.45` in the strong-disturbance setting and keeps `min_h > 0`. The moderate and tight actuation-limit cases also keep positive margins with small tracking-error changes. | Supported by robustness checks. | The severe-limit case is a boundary case: tracking collapses under full thrust saturation, so do not describe it as successful tracking. |
| The acceleration-level theorem supports forward invariance and bounded tracking. | Theorem 1 states invariance under QP feasibility, bounded disturbance-estimation error, and bounded execution error with a robust CBF margin. Theorem 2 now states UUB using a linear error-system Lyapunov equation and an explicit residual-set bound. `output/6dof_batch/safety_filter_diagnostics.csv` reports 100% numerical QP success in the tested CBF cases, active-filter rates of 14.6%-24.3%, and maximum acceleration correction of 0.580 m/s^2. | Supported analytically under assumptions, with simulation diagnostics for the tested cases. | Do not extend this proof to PX4 internal controllers. Theorem 2 gives bounded tracking, not asymptotic convergence during active safety filtering. The diagnostics do not prove universal feasibility. |
| PX4 SITL + Gazebo supports practical implementability. | `output/px4_offboard_experiments/summary.csv` includes five scenarios. In single-obstacle, large-obstacle, multi-obstacle, and wind-bias runs, `baseline_no_cbf` reaches negative `min_h` values (`-0.567941`, `-1.029714`, `-0.537423`, `-1.278094`), while `cbf_velocity_filter` remains positive (`0.215137`, `0.024507`, `0.080774`, `0.586612`). `multi_scenario_trajectories.png` shows the corresponding path reshaping around obstacle sets. | Supported as flight-stack validation. | Current PX4 test uses a velocity-level filter. The wind-bias test is a command-level disturbance proxy, not a physical Gazebo wind field, and the tuned CBF run preserves safety by accepting a larger final goal distance. The narrow-passage run is a feasibility case because both controllers remain safe. Do not claim full-stack forward invariance. |

## Main rejection risks to address next

1. The manuscript still needs more recent and journal-specific UAV/CBF references, preferably including papers from `Aerospace Science and Technology`, `Aerospace`, `Drones`, `IEEE Transactions on Control Systems Technology`, `IEEE Transactions on Aerospace and Electronic Systems`, or closely related venues.
2. The PX4/Gazebo validation is stronger after adding multi-scenario, trajectory, and wind-bias stress evidence, but it is still a velocity-level offboard filter. A reviewer may ask for acceleration/thrust-level offboard commands, moving obstacles, or a physical wind-field PX4/Gazebo run.
3. The Elsevier `elsarticle` draft compiles, but the final submission system may require separate `highlights`, figure-source files, graphical abstract if requested, and declaration forms.
4. Some BibTeX entries are arXiv/manual references. These should be verified against final published versions where available.
